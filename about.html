Strategic MEL Leader

The Strategist and The Technologist

I am Reagan Ochola, a Strategic Monitoring, Evaluation, and Learning (MEL) Leader who believes that the most powerful data is the data that drives immediate, measurable action. For over nine years, I have specialized in bridging the critical gap between high-level donor strategy and the complex reality of data collection in multi-country development programs. My work is dedicated to transforming raw field data into clear, actionable evidence that enhances program sustainability and impact. My approach is defined by a commitment to efficiency and precision. By automating data pipelines and optimizing MEL systems, I have consistently delivered tangible results, including cutting reporting turnaround time by 50% and reducing survey errors by 35%. I translate these efficiencies into strategic gains, such as the 8.1% training-efficiency gain identified in the Kenya MSME Manufacturing Analysis, ensuring every program resource is directed by rigorous evidence.

From Frameworks to Full-Stack Analytics

My career began with a deep focus on designing robust, donor-compliant MEL frameworks. I learned firsthand that a well-designed framework is the bedrock of sustainable development, but I also witnessed the limitations of manual processes when managing 50+ staff and vast, complex datasets. This challenge sparked a pivotal transition in my professional journey. Driven by the need for agility and precision, I embraced the power of data science. I mastered Power BI for developing real-time, interactive dashboards that serve as the single source of truth for executive leadership and field teams alike. I leverage SQL to build scalable, reliable data infrastructure and Python for advanced data cleaning, geospatial analysis, and reproducible research. Today, I operate at the intersection of strategy and technology, designing adaptive MEL systems that are not only compliant but are also fully automated and directly feed into evidence-based decision-making.

Data Must Serve the Mission

My professional philosophy is simple and unwavering: Data must serve the mission. It is not enough to collect data; we must translate it into the clearest possible narrative to drive change. Every framework, every dashboard, and every analysis I build is designed to make the 'learning' in MEL a proactive, continuous process. I am motivated by the challenge of scaling data-driven MEL across complex, multi-stakeholder environments. I am focused on building resilient, self-sustaining systems that move organizations past simply measuring outputs and towards a deep, evidence-based understanding of why and how change occurs. If you are looking for a leader who can bridge the gap between program strategy and technical data execution to maximize impact, I invite you to explore my portfolio.
